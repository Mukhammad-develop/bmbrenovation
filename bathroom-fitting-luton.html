Victorian and Edwardian terraces in Luton — particularly in Bury Park, the town centre, and surrounding streets — were built without dedicated bathrooms. The bathroom was retrofitted later, usually into a back bedroom or a rear extension, so the position of soil stacks, waste outlets, and pipework varies considerably from house to house. Original cast iron stacks have often been adapted multiple times with a mix of materials. Pipework in these properties can be a combination of lead, copper, and later plastic runs — sometimes in surprising positions. Floor joists in older terraces can also be undersized for heavier items like cast iron or acrylic baths and may need reinforcing. None of this prevents a great result — but all of it needs to be identified before work starts. We check construction type, substrate, and drainage layout during the free home visit and factor any specific requirements into the written quote upfront, so there are no surprises once work begins.
